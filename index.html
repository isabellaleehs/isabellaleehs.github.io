About

I am a current student at The University of Chicago, and was born and raised in Singapore. Prior to taking an interest in Economics and finance, I was a devoted Science student, participating in multiple Math and Science olympiads, obsessing over neuroscience, doing research, placing at the Google Science Fair and thriving within an enriched environment in the Raffles Academy (Biology) at Raffles Institution (Junior College). Although I still harbor a deep interest in the Sciences, I have decided to pursue a different field in college, in an effort to understand the bigger picture of how socioeconomic forces shape societal advancements, and begin to grasp how private capital can truly make a tangible difference across fields like academia and infrastructure development.

School aside, I am a self-professed balletomane and am deeply interested and involved in the arts. I have been training in classical ballet since I was 3 years old, and formally training in contemporary dance since the age of 13, performing on numerous stages at a pre-professional level. I enjoy taking dance classes wherever I go, improvising, catching Broadway shows, and doing Swing dancing when I have the time. I also love netball, kayaking, sport-climbing and trekking up mountain trails -- I hope to be able to visit all of America's national parks in my lifetime! Most recently, I have taken an interest in programming, and am in the midst of self-learning front-end web development, Python, R and Stata. Check out some of my projects below!
