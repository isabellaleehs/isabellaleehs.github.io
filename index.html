About

I am a current student at The University of Chicago majoring in Economics and Statistics, and completing a joint Master's degree in Computer Science in my fourth year of college. My interests lie in research, technology and the markets, which has led me to pursue opportunities at the cross section of Economics, data science and technology. I absolutely love problem-solving and being challenged, and am excited to pursue my interests in fixed income research after graduation.

My time in college has led me to research roles in academia, the U.S. Peace Corps and in finance, and I've loved being able to develop an affinity for quantitative approaches to understanding the real world across various fields. Pursuing Computer Science has additionally allowed me to develop a stronger skill set with regard to the understanding and development of software used to solve problems in a variety of contexts, and I'm eager to put these skills to use in understanding the markets. That said, I've always had a passion for education, and am always looking for ways that I can integrate these interests. Having spent the better half of my high school and college careers tutoring students who struggle with school or working on education-related research, I'd like to see how I can use technology or technology education to improve classroom performance and financial literacy in schools.

Outside of school, I am deeply involved in the dance world. Having trained in classical ballet and contemporary dance from babyhood, I have been fortunate to have performed on numerous stages at a pre-professional and professional level and participate in the field across different cities over the years. I am a current co-Director of UChicago Maya (a UChicago dance company), and make it a point to take dance classes wherever I go. I also love netball, kayaking, sport-climbing and hiking -- I hope to be able to visit all of America's national parks in my lifetime! My most recent hobby has been programming - I picked it up in the summer of 2017, attended HackMIT and was hooked, and that turned into my Master's in CS. I am currently well-versed in Python and R, with experience in front-end web development, SQL and some mobile software development. It's been a constant learning process, and that's what's so exciting about this field. Check out some of my other projects below!

Tweet Frequency Analysis

This mini project draws inspiration from one of the twitter analysis projects I completed in my CMSC 12100 class in fall 2017. The class assignment focused on analyzing UK election tweets, first by finding top frequencies of tweet entities, then by pre-processing tweet texts and analyzing them in the form of ngrams.

The project finds the top 10 hashtags of two Twitter handles, and displays them according to frequency in the form of a bar chart. The code can be easily modified to analyze other Twitter entities like user mentions, or tweet text (although an additional pre-processing step is needed). Any number of handles can be analyzed, and different charts created accordingly. I'm planning to expand on this in future (hence the numerous files) with other forms of tweet analysis and data visualization.

Primary Language: Python

APIs: Vincent, Tweepy

  • Date: December 2017
  • Service: Data Analysis

Choropleth Map

This code creates a choropleth map visualizing the number of people of all ages living with HIV in the world, mapped by country. Simply run 'make_map.py' to open the map in your browzer. The bulk of the work was in cleaning the data; Plotly was relatively easy to use. I'm still on the hunt for a Python library that has the visual capabilities of R!

Data: World Health Organization

Primary Language: Python

APIs: Pandas, Plotly, Pycountry

  • Date: December 2017

Discover Dish

Discover Dish is a web application that aims to make trying new cuisines less intimidating for users, by providing more personalized restaurant recommendations using collaborative filtering. The app outputs recommendations that the user is likely to enjoy, while also ensuring a minimum cultural distance from the user's input restaurant, so as to allow the user to experience new cuisines. I loved making this web app happen, and my team was amazing to work with!

Primary Language: Python, Django (Web Framework)

APIs: Geoplotlib, BeautifulSoup4, Django Crispy Forms

  • Date: February-March 2017
  • Service: Web scraping, Web app development

Belay Messaging Web App

Belay is a database-backed single-page web application that hosts messaging chatrooms. Users can create or enter existing chatrooms, and can create messages or comment threads. They also undergo authentication and are able to update personal login information in the database. This was created in an MPCS class.

Primary Language: Python, HTML, CSS, JS, SQL

APIs: SendGrid

  • Date: February-March 2019
  • Service: Web app development
